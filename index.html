THE BOARD
— as we see it —

Welcome to the 2026 S'feld Scouting Services NFL Draft Preview — our projected First Round, built pick by pick with the unvarnished scouting takes our readers have come to expect. What follows is not a mock draft in the conventional sense. It is an intelligence brief. A war-room whisper. A reading of where the board breaks, where trades shake loose, and where value quietly hides in plain sight.

We project four in-round trades, a quarterback going No. 1, a record-tying offensive-line run, and at least one "did-they-just-do-that?" swing in the middle of Round 1. Each pick is paired with The Extra Point — our bonus take on the coach, executive, or franchise narrative riding shotgun with the selection.

As always, Trust the Tape.

01
On the Clock
Las Vegas Raiders
NEED · QBSSS GRADE · A−

Fernando Mendoza

QB Indiana

In a draft that will see lots of drama within the first 10 selections in terms of how it shapes the rest of the night, there is no drama here as Las Vegas finally gets its chance to be relevant at the most important position selecting Indiana quarterback Fernando Mendoza with the No. 1 overall pick. The Raiders find themselves in a position they have rarely occupied - holding the keys to the draft - and Mendoza represents the type of foundational swing the franchise has long needed to take. In the same way the Bears and Commanders were offered a plethora of picks to move out of the #1 and #2 spots two years ago, the Raiders realize that extra capital is not worth potentially landing the next big thing. With a blend of size and mobility, Mendoza checks all the boxes scouts love, and for a team that has cycled through stopgap solutions under center, this is the kind of bet that simply must be made. That said, SSS believes that history tempers the excitement. While most pundits have labeled Mendoza a can't-miss prospect, S'feld is not quite ready to echo that level of certainty. The Raiders' track record with high-leverage decisions leaves little room for blind optimism, and even the most polished quarterback prospects are not immune to organizational instability. We have always said that it isn't always when you go – but where you go – and Mendoza is leaving a guy in Curt Cignetti who never misses for an organization that rarely hits. Here is hoping that having the biggest winner in NFL history in Tom Brady overseeing things in Las Vegas changes that narrative and allows Mendoza to expand his college success to the professional ranks. Mendoza may very well possess the tools to alter the trajectory of the franchise, but expecting an immediate turnaround ignores the organizational chaos and impatience that has plagued Las Vegas for years. This is most definitely the correct move to make, but not a guaranteed remedy for what has long been an ailment for the Raiders.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 01

With Tom Brady now firmly embedded in the Raiders' decision-making structure, this pick carries an added layer of intrigue. Brady's résumé as a player is untouchable, but projecting quarterback success from the front office is an entirely different challenge. By aligning himself with Mendoza, Brady is effectively staking his executive credibility on this evaluation. It's a fascinating watch in the same way that we watched Michael Jordan try but ultimately fail to bring excellence to the Charlotte Hornets after his career was over. The transition from greatest player to greatest executive is rarely achieved as the success and control these players had on the field or court is rarely replicated in the office. And for Brady, he is taking on what of the most challenging franchises in the NFL. In some ways, Mendoza's development will reflect not just on the Raiders' future, but on Brady's evolving legacy in the next phase of his football life.

On the Clock
New York Jets
NEED · EDGE/LBSSS GRADE · A

Arvell Reese

OLB Ohio State

While much of the conversation has centered around Texas Tech's David Bailey for this spot, Ohio State OLB Arvell Reese brings a different profile that will ultimately win out in the Jets war room. Though Reese carries a slightly smaller build than Bailey, he is widely regarded as the stronger of the two, and that strength shows up consistently at the point of attack as Reese consistently wins the physics battle. He plays like a coiled spring, converting power through contact, and that translates into stack-and-shed ability that pops on tape. He's not a traditional edge-only defender - Reese operates primarily as an off-ball linebacker who can seamlessly moonlight as a pass rusher, giving New York a chess piece rather than a single-position player. What separates Reese is his ability to impact every level of the defense and operate as a true weapon who can be deployed all over the field depending on the situation and matchup. His 251 coverage snaps showcase that versatility as that number that placed him among the most active second-level defenders in space in this class. Reese shows comfort dropping into zones, matching routes and closing windows with urgency. He can be used as a blitzer while maintaining a run-stop percentage that reflects his strength at the point of attack. Reese is a "do more of more" defender and someone who expands the playbook rather than merely fitting into it. The defensive equivalent of a utility knife, Reese is reliable across multiple situations, giving coordinators flexibility that offenses struggle to account for snap-to-snap.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 02

Head coach Aaron Glenn enters a pivotal second season after what can only be described as a disastrous first year at the helm. The margin for patience is gone and the pressure to prove he can lead this team is very real. Drafting a player like Reese would be like handing a builder high-end tools but what matters now is whether Glenn can actually construct something stable. Year two isn't just about marginal improvement, it's about establishing an actual direction and identity which was why they hired him from the Lions. If Reese becomes the initial tone-setter in a draft where the Jets have a lot of opportunities, it will go a long way in re-establishing Glenn's vision, but if the same issues persist, even high-end talent won't be enough to quiet the noise surrounding his future.

New York Giants
NEED · RBSSS GRADE · A

Jeremiyah Love

RB Notre Dame

Are we ready to have some fun? NFL insider Jay Glazer says he is confident of a major unforeseen trade going down tonight at the draft, but he can't disclose it. Well unlike Glazer, S'feld Services actually tells you what it is going to be! We project the New York Giants to move up two spots striking a deal with the Cardinals for the No. 3 overall pick, targeting Notre Dame RB Jeremiyah Love. Rather than sitting back and letting the draft come to them, the Giants and new head coach John Harbaugh get ahead of the posse and secure the consensus top tailback in the class before another team can make a similar move. Love profiles as a true workhorse - built for volume, yet explosive enough to flip the field in an instant. His production backs it up, averaging over 6.0 yards per carry while handling a heavy workload, and forcing missed tackles at a rate that consistently tilted drives in Notre Dame's favor. He runs with a downhill temperament but has the burst of a home-run hitter, the kind of dual-threat presence that can turn a routine first down into a momentum-shifting play. The vision here is clear - Harbaugh is looking to recreate the formula that brought sustained success during his time in Baltimore. That blueprint revolves around a dominant ground game anchored by a bell cow back - much like Harbaugh had with Derrick Henry at his peak - paired with a young quarterback who benefits from structure and balance. For second-year QB Jaxson Dart, Love becomes more than just a weapon, he's a stabilizer who can open up the offense. A reliable run game shortens third downs, slows pass rushes and simplifies coverages, all of which will accelerate Dart's development. SSS gets the sense the Giants are looking to make multiple a big splash in the 2026 Draft and no longer wade in the shallow end of the pool as they have the past several years.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 03

The Giants aren't just investing in Love, they're getting out of their own way and letting John Harbaugh attempt to restore credibility to a franchise that has drifted well below the league's standard of mediocrity. Ownership is signaling urgency and this aggressive trade-up reflects a belief that Harbaugh's culture can quickly recalibrate expectations. More importantly, Harbaugh represents the closest thing the Giants have had in years to the Bill Parcells/Tom Coughlin model which was built on a punishing run game and hard-nosed defense, which, not coincidentally, delivered the franchise's last Super Bowl titles.

On the Clock
Tennessee Titans
NEED · EDGESSS GRADE · A−

David Bailey

OLB Texas Tech

In a spot where the board could break a dozen different ways, SSS feels Tennessee will stay put at No. 4 and select Texas Tech OLB David Bailey. The Titans are expected to field calls here, mirroring the activity around Arizona a pick earlier, from teams looking to jump the line, but the decision ultimately settles into a two-man debate: Bailey versus Miami's Rueben Bain Jr. Once the Giants swooped in for Jeremiyah Love, the "shiny object" offensive option disappeared, and Tennessee's focus snapped back to defense. This roster has more issues than a single running back could mask and adding a back would be like putting premium tires on a Yugo. Bailey, on the other hand provides edge disruption and gives the Titans a tone-setter who can immediately impact opposing quarterbacks. Bailey's appeal lies in his ability to consistently collapse the pocket and affect protection schemes. He brings a blend of length, burst and strength that shows up in both his pressure rate and his ability to convert speed to power, traits that separate good pass rushers from great ones. While Bain offers a compelling case with his polish, Bailey projects a higher-upside and is the kind of defender who can tilt a game with a strip-sack or force hurried throws that lead to turnovers. Tennessee's defense has lacked that kind of presence off the edge and Bailey fills that void in a way that aligns with building from the trenches outward. Robert Saleh got run out of New York on a rail because he couldn't figure out the offensive side of the ball, now he is getting back to his roots and building a defense that will allow QB Cam Ward to have a higher margin for error.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 04

After a turbulent run with the Jets, Saleh now inherits a Titans franchise where ownership involvement has been known to muddy long-term vision. The lesson here is clear - Saleh will need to apply what he learned in New York, particularly around managing expectations, staff cohesion and offensive support to avoid repeating the same cycle. It's his chance for a second act where the script is familiar, but the execution must be sharper. If Bailey becomes the defensive cornerstone he's projected to be, it gives Saleh a building block - but turning that into sustained success will depend on whether he can navigate both the locker room and the front office with greater control this time around.

Arizona Cardinals
NEED · OTSSS GRADE · A

Francis Mauigoa

OT Miami (FL)

SSS feels strongly that the Arizona will make the correct decision and trade out of the No. 3 overall pick, leveraging their draft position to stockpile capital and address multiple roster deficiencies. In a brutally competitive NFC West, where stacking wins is a steep climb for a roster still in transition, GM Monti Ossenfort appears to be committing to a longer horizon rather than chasing short-term relevance. With one of the worst quarterback situations in the NFL, the logic is clear - take the best offensive tackle on the board in Francis Mauigoa, fortify the trenches and accumulate the draft ammunition needed to make a more informed swing at a franchise QB in what projects to be a far deeper quarterback class next year. The added flexibility could even allow Arizona to potentially double dip on trade-down opportunities as SSS sees a window for Ossenfort to move out of the 1st Round twice if the board breaks favorably. That kind of maneuvering would turn one premium pick into a multi-year roster-building toolkit, which is exactly the kind of asset management a rebuilding team needs when it's not ready to compete. Mauigoa becomes the initial move on the chessboard, an anchor at tackle who can protect whatever young passer eventually takes over. In today's age where instant gratification seems to be the only goal, the Cards instead compound value, turning a single chip into multiple hands at the table instead of going all-in too early.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 05

SSS has long believed Ossenfort was the right hire to reshape the Cardinals' long-term direction, but the inherited limitations of quarterback Kyler Murray slowed the timeline and complicated the evaluation process. In many ways, Ossenfort has been forced to build around a mismatch between his vision and roster reality. That tension has now led to a noticeable pivot in philosophy and staffing direction - moving from a defensive-minded hire in Jonathan Gannon to an offensive-driven approach with Mike LaFleur. It's a clear 180, signaling that Arizona is finally prioritizing quarterback development and offensive structure as the central pillar of its rebuild rather than trying to retrofit a defense-first identity onto an offense that hasn't had stability at its core.

On the Clock
Cleveland Browns
NEED · OLSSS GRADE · A−

Spencer Fano

OL Utah

There is a lot on the line for the Browns in this draft and SSS trusts that Cleveland will ultimately resist the temptation to trade out and instead stay put to select Utah offensive lineman Spencer Fano. While many around the league continue to assume this pick is available, Cleveland's internal math appears to be shifting toward stability over speculation. Fano isn't the kind of pick that will drive jersey sales or dominates highlight reels, but he represents something far more important for this roster. In a division where defensive fronts routinely dictate games, the Browns appear ready to build from the inside out rather than fall for the trap of chasing noise at the top of the draft. This is also where new head coach Todd Monken becomes central to the decision. Monken understands that quarterback development is less about flash and more about foundation, and Fano gives Cleveland exactly that. He is a tone-setting presence who can anchor protection schemes and reduce chaos for whoever ends up under center. Fano's strength at the point of attack and consistency in pass sets immediately raises the floor of the entire offense, and in a division defined by elite defenses, that kind of reliability matters more than skill position glamour.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 06

For several years, S'feld Services has been vocal in the belief that Todd Monken deserved a legitimate opportunity as a head coach, and Cleveland has now handed him that chance. This is a franchise that has too often cycled through leadership. Monken, however, brings a different tone - disciplined, detail-oriented and rooted in physical football. If there is any coach positioned to reconnect this team with the identity of the Dawg Pound, it's him. The selection of Fano would be an extension of that philosophy: less flash, more fight and a commitment to let everyone know who was letting the dogs out by building a roster that reflects toughness first.

On the Clock
Washington Commanders
NEED · SSSS GRADE · A+

Caleb Downs

S Ohio State

Washington may be sitting down the board at Pick #7, but S'feld is confident the Commanders are about to land one of our favorite players in the entire draft with Ohio State safety Caleb Downs. Downs isn't getting the proper credit he is deserved simply because he plays a position that is rarely coveted in the top 10. SSS places him firmly in the top-three talent tier, as he is a player whose tape consistently looks like a veteran operating in a rookie's body. From his early impact at Alabama to his immediate transition and dominance at Ohio State, Downs has done one thing at every stop: exceed expectations from Day 1 without needing an adjustment period. That kind of instant translation is rare, especially in the secondary, where most players need time for the game to slow down. What separates him from others is how often he shows up when the game is on the line. He is a safety in label only as he functions as a hybrid agitator who runs downhill like a linebacker and closes space like a corner. Against the run, he doesn't just fit gaps, he detonates them, routinely finishing plays in the backfield and forcing offenses to reconfigure protection schemes just to account for his alignment. Of the teams picking in front of Washington - pass on the OSU safety at your own risk because the arrow is pointing up on Downs.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 07

For Commanders GM Adam Peters, the decision comes down to one principle - don't overthink the label, evaluate the player. Downs is not merely a defensive back, he is a defensive centerpiece who expands what an entire scheme can become. Limiting his potential through the safety label is like limiting a great cheesecake simply because it comes after a ribeye. Downs is so good that teams won't realize until it is too late that the dessert was actually the main course.

New Orleans Saints
NEED · DESSS GRADE · B+

Rueben Bain Jr.

DE Miami (FL)

SSS feels this could be one of the most fascinating upside bets in the top 10 with New Orleans picking up Miami (FL) defensive end Rueben Bain Jr. at No. 8. While Caleb Downs landing in Washington remains SSS's favorite pick in the draft, Bain may be the most intriguing swing due to the sheer volatility of his ceiling. New Orleans' defense is in the middle of a necessary recalibration, and whether or not veteran Cam Jordan returns, the reality remains the same - the Saints need youth, burst, and chaos off the edge. Bain fits that mold as a potential tone-shifter for the front seven. What makes Bain so compelling is that he wins in ways that don't always fit the traditional prototype. Yes, the arm length concerns are real on paper, but they rarely show up as a limitation on tape where his power profile takes over. And as S'feld always says - "Trust the Tape." He plays with the torque of a defensive tackle and the first-step explosion of a pure edge rusher, creating a unique profile that offensive tackles struggle to time. Hurricane Bain was a battering ram at Miami showcasing inside counters, sudden leverage shifts and violent hands. Even when blockers think they've anchored, Bain has a way of collapsing space like a closing elevator door. For a Saints defense that has leaned too heavily on an aging front, Bain represents the kind of controlled chaos that can reset how they play up front.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 08

SSS believes both GM Mickey Loomis and second-year head coach Kellen Moore deserve a ton of credit for showing conviction last year when they selected QB Tyler Shough in the second round despite widespread skepticism around the league. That pick, viewed as a reach by many evaluators, is now being lauded as the Saints willingness to trust their internal grading over consensus opinion. For a position as important as QB, New Orleans gave themselves a major boost if Shough continues to play like he did in 2025. And don't be surprised if the Saints trade up in this draft as they have made 25 draft day trades since 2008 and have traded up all 25 times.

On the Clock
Kansas City Chiefs
NEED · CBSSS GRADE · A

Mansoor Delane

CB LSU

The Chiefs' secondary has undergone a sweeping reset this offseason, with multiple starters departing and the unit now requiring meaningful, high-end reinforcements. While Andy Reid and GM Brett Veach have already begun patching holes through free agency, that scale of turnover makes it clear that one offseason alone won't be enough. In that context, landing the top corner on the board in LSU CB Mansoor Delane becomes less of a luxury and more of a necessity - especially for a defense that will be asked to carry more weight while the offense transitions. Delane fits that need with both production and pedigree. A first-team All-SEC performer and finalist for the Jim Thorpe Award, he tied for ninth in the FBS with 1.2 passes defended per game, consistently disrupting timing-based passing attacks in the rugged SEC. He plays with the instincts of a seasoned NFL corner already, showing strong route anticipation and an ability to close throwing windows before quarterbacks fully commit. Given the Chiefs' significant turnover at corner, losing both starting outside roles through a combination of trade and free agency, the selection of Delane is the right move despite the fact the Chiefs may be tempted to add offensive weapons drafting this high.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 09

SSS is particularly interested to see whether Kansas City resists the temptation to "get cute" at No. 9, a draft slot they have rarely occupied in recent years given their consistent Super Bowl contention. This is unfamiliar territory for a franchise that has spent most of the last decade drafting at the back of rounds, not the top 10. The question becomes one of discipline - will Andy Reid prioritize a clear defensive need with a top-tier corner like Delane or will his long-standing offensive creativity pull him toward the allure of a skill position? It's a battle between logic to draft defense or the dopamine urge to add offense. For a team trying to sustain dominance while retooling on defense, the answer may define how this next Chiefs era begins.

On the Clock
New York Giants
NEED · LBSSS GRADE · A

Sonny Styles

LB Ohio State

In one of the most balanced first-round strategies in the draft, the New York Giants follow up their selection of offensive dynamo Jeremiyah Love at No. 3 with Ohio State linebacker Sonny Styles at No. 10. If Love is the spark plug on offense, Styles is the engine on defense and this pick brings symmetry to the roster. Styles dramatically elevated his draft standing after one of the most memorable scouting combine performances in recent memory, where his movement skills at his size created a ripple effect across front offices. The combine highlighted his range and fluidity of a safety with the frame of a linebacker. Styles would immediately slot alongside recent free-agent acquisition Tremaine Edmunds, giving the Giants a long, physical, and versatile second level capable of reshaping how they defend the run. Last season's run defense ranked among the league's worst in yards before contact allowed, functioning more like a leaky dam than a front seven, but Styles brings the kind of downhill closing speed and block-shedding ability that can help turn that weakness into a strength. In many ways, this pairing begins to resemble a more Raven-like blueprint under John Harbaugh's influence - size, speed, and physicality layered in the middle of the defense to control tempo and force offenses into predictable situations. If the draft were to fall this way through 10 picks, the Giants would be putting the rest of the NFC East on notice.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 10

By pairing Love's explosive, space-creating offense with Styles' field-destroying defensive range, the Giants are effectively attacking both sides of the ball with two of the most dynamic players in the draft. Love provides the rhythm while Styles provides the resistance, and together they would create a framework where the Giants are no longer reacting to terms, but dictating them.

Miami Dolphins
NEED · OTSSS GRADE · A

Kadyn Proctor

OT Alabama

It's obvious that Miami is leaning into a full-scale reset, and for the Dolphins it begins by selecting Alabama offensive tackle Kadyn Proctor to protect newly signed free-agent quarterback Malik Willis. After effectively stripping the roster down to the studs, Miami enters this draft with more needs than almost any team in the league, and the new regime of GM Jon-Eric Sullivan and head coach Jeff Hafley appears aligned on a simple truth that you don't try to fix everything at once, you start inside and work outward. Proctor brings the rare physical profile teams dream of - massive frame, light feet for his size, and the raw anchor strength to immediately stabilize the edge of the offensive line. Hafley's influence is already visible in how this rebuild is being framed. Drawing from his Green Bay experience, there is a clear attempt to replicate a "Lambeau Southeast" identity - physical upfront, disciplined and built to develop quarterbacks rather than cycle through them. In that model, Proctor will be a tone-setter for the entire offensive rebuild. Miami's AFC East climb will not be immediate, but this is patience with a purpose. The best organizations know you protect the quarterback, stabilize the trenches and gradually layer in skill players once the foundation is secure.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 11

While Miami still has a long road back to contention, SSS notes that the decisiveness of the front office's early moves is significant. The pairing of Jon-Eric Sullivan and Jeff Hafley signals a clean break from the past, and perhaps more importantly, the acquisition of Malik Willis at a below-market cost reflects a belief that more than just the GM and Coach have bought in. In a league where quarterback prices dictate roster flexibility, Miami's ability to secure Willis without overextending resources gives them breathing room to build properly around him.

Dallas Cowboys
NEED · DESSS GRADE · B

Keldric Faulk

DE Auburn

SSS believes Jerry Jones will once again go off the reservation and select Auburn edge rusher Keldric Faulk with the No. 12 overall pick. While most mainstream media types don't have Faulk anywhere near this range, let alone inside the top 20, Dallas has rarely operated in lockstep with consensus, especially when traits outweigh production in their evaluations. The Cowboys enter this draft needing a defensive resurgence after surrendering the most points in the NFL last season, and Faulk offers exactly the kind of traits-based upside that tends to appeal to their war room with his long frame, explosive first step, and the ability to convert speed to power off the edge. Faulk's 2025 season wasn't dominant by any means, but this projection is built on what he can be, not what he currently is. In a different system, he might be viewed as a developmental piece, but Dallas often bets on ceiling over certainty, and Faulk's upside could be top-10 caliber if everything clicks. It should be known that the Cowboys have had a pretty good track record selecting disruptive pass rushing forces at Pick 12, however, as they selected Micah Parsons at Pick #12 five years ago. If Faulk is even half as good as the previous #12 selection, this pick is a home run.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 12

For all their flaws, the Cowboys have historically managed to stay competitive, even in seasons where the team felt uneven. However, there is growing concern that they are trending toward a third straight losing season, something the franchise has not experienced since the Dave Campo era from 2000 - 2002. The common denominator across much of Jerry Jones' tenure has been a tendency to do less with more - assembling talent without always achieving results. That puts added pressure on head coach Brian Schottenheimer, who now finds himself in the difficult position of not only maximizing a roster, but also navigating the influence of an owner known for thinking too far outside the box. Will Dallas finally align potential with production, or will it be the same circus, different clowns?

Los Angeles Rams
NEED · OTSSS GRADE · B+

Monroe Freeling

OT Georgia

It won't be popular opinion, but the Los Angeles Rams will pivot away from popular first-round speculation - ranging from USC receiver Makai Lemon to Tennessee corner Colton Hood - and instead secure Georgia offensive tackle Monroe Freeling with the No. 13 overall pick. While the consensus outside the building leans toward adding another weapon for the passing game, Los Angeles appears to be thinking several moves ahead. With the potential for a significant portion of their offensive line to hit free agency next offseason, the Rams are prioritizing insulation over excitement. Freeling represents exactly that kind of forward-looking investment. A long, technically refined tackle with light feet and the ability to mirror edge rushers in pass protection, he profiles as a player who can stabilize the blind side if developed properly. While wide receiver remains a tempting narrative given the Rams' history of uncovering gems like Puka Nacua, it just isn't the responsible play here and the Rams can attack that position down the board. The Freeling pick is about preserving Stafford for as long as possible and the system that makes the passing game function at a high level in the first place.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 13

Around league circles, Sean McVay is increasingly viewed as the NFL's closest modern equivalent to Bill Walsh, not just for offensive innovation but for the coaching ecosystem he has created. Every offseason, teams circle his staff and tree searching for the next schematic architect, much like how Walsh's influence once spread across the league through generations of assistants. McVay has shown he can zig when others zag and reinvent himself and his team to maximize where his strengths lie.

Baltimore Ravens
NEED · OGSSS GRADE · A−

Olaivavega Ioane

OG Penn State

Offensive line continues to be the theme as Baltimore's opts for Penn State offensive guard Olaivavega Ioane at #14. While flashier skill-position options may often dominate early in drafts, Baltimore's approach remains rooted in trench strength. This would mark the first time in franchise history that the Ravens have used a top-half first-round pick on a guard, underscoring just how urgent the need has become to provide further protection for QB Lamar Jackson. Ioane profiles as the best interior lineman in the class, bringing elite anchor strength and consistency, having not surrendered a sack over his last two seasons - quite an impressive feat. Jackson endured a 37.1% pressure rate in 2025, the second worst mark in the league, a number that essentially forced Baltimore's hand in addressing protection from the inside out. Ioane offers immediate help in reducing pocket collapse from the A-gaps, allowing Baltimore to reestablish timing and spacing in the passing game. It's a pick that won't lead Sportscenter, but it gives the Ravens a player who excels in a physical run scheme, and you are never going to go wrong making picks to keep Lamar Jackson upright.

◆ The Extra Point FILE NO. 14

Owner Steve Bisciotti recently faced the difficult reality of moving on from a coach who had been part of the organization for an extraordinary 18 years. That kind of institutional continuity is rare in modern sports, and its end signals a turning point for the franchise. Around the division, it also marks the fading of an era defined by legendary AFC North battles, as both Mike Tomlin's long tenure in Pittsburgh and John Harbaugh's historic run in Baltimore now feel like overlapping chapters nearing their conclusion. For nearly two decades, those rivalries shaped the identity of the division; now, with both pillars shifting, the Ravens' next era begins not just with new personnel, but with a changing of the guard across the entire AFC North landscape.

On the Clock
Tampa Bay Buccaneers
NEED · EDGESSS GRADE · A−

Akheem Mesidor

DE Miami (FL)

Improving the pass rush is not optional for Todd Bowles' defense, it's a major necessity and Akheem Mesidor offers exactly the type of ready-made pass rusher the roster currently lacks. He is a polished, high-motor defender with a deep arsenal of pass-rush moves, violent hands and the kind of change-of-direction burst that allows him to win both around and through offensive tackles. In a defense that often asks its front to create pressure without heavy blitz reliance, Mesidor would be ready for action from Day 1.
